Here we are discussing strategies for each grade!

Freshman year (9th grade)

If you are a freshmen, this are things you should consider!

    -Maintain Strong Academics

    -Build Relationships by contacting alumni from your area

    -Get a LinkedIn account

    -Get Involved in Extracurricular Activities

    -Pursue Your Interests

    -Meet with counselor and tell them your goals

    -Plan for Summer Activities

    -Look for opportunities for next years

Sophomore year (10th garde)

If you are a sophomore, this are things you should consider!

    -Build a Well-Rounded Transcript

    -Extracurricular Activities:Leadership roles

    -Summer Programs and/or internships

    -Develop Strong Writing and Communication Skills

    -Build Relationships with Teachers for recommendations

    -Standardized Tests: SAT or ACT

    -College Research

    -Financial Planning and family conversation

Junior year (11th grade)

If you are a junior, this are things you should consider as you come closer to college!

    -Standardized Testing:Take or retake SAT or ACT

    -show Leadership and initiative in your activities

    -Community Involvement

    -Decide who will write your Letters of Recommendations

    -Build Relationships with Teachers for recommendations

    -Research Schools

    -Brainstorm Personal Statement/Essays

    -Set goals and meet with mentors and counselor

Senior (12th grade)

If you are a senior, this are things you should consider as you apply to college!

    -Financial Aid and Scholarships: They are many scholarships for FGLI students and any types of students!

    -College list

    -Showcase Your Unique Qualities

    -Demonstrate Passion and Commitment

    -Get letters of recommendation

    -Write Personal Statement/Essays

    -Sort deadlines

    -Get all the documents and test scores needed

    -Set goals and meet with mentors and counselor to review your application!

Scholarships strategy

Find scholarships that apply to you!

1. The more unique and niche, the better. For example, scholarships only for students in your age group, State, City, Field of interest, etc. The less competitive the better!

2. Reuse and repurpose essays.

Most scholarships have similar prompts. You can use the structure of previous essays to answer others.

3. Letters of recommendation

Have one teacher in specific write you a letter of recommendation that can be reused.

4. Sort deadlines

Sorting datelines will help you stay on top of things and get all the documents and essays on time.
